# VoltSol Repositioning Spec — "Make It. Store It. Use It." (CANONICAL)
**Authoritative terminology + legal rules for the off-grid → residential self-consumption refactor.**
Every page, content file, and Ray's brain must conform. 2026-06-20.

## The core thesis
VoltSol installs **residential solar + battery storage** for Northern California homeowners who want to
**make their own power, store it, and use it themselves** — instead of sending it to the utility for a
fraction of what the utility charges to sell it back. Energy independence + blackout resilience.

## Terminology mapping (find → replace intent)
- "off-grid solar" (as the PRODUCT/brand frame) → **"residential solar + battery storage"** /
  **"solar with battery storage"** / **"home solar + storage"**
- "off-grid" as a benefit → **"energy independence"**, **"self-powered home"**, **"use your own power"**,
  **"keep the power you make"**
- "off-grid capable" → **"battery-backed / self-consumption"** or **"runs your home from your own battery"**
- KEEP "off-grid" ONLY where literally accurate: true remote/rural builds with no/expensive grid access
  (e.g. the rural-foothill article may keep it where it means genuinely disconnected). Even there, lead
  with self-consumption framing; off-grid is the edge case, not the headline.
- Do NOT delete the mini-split from the PRODUCT (pricing tiers include it). But it is a COMPONENT, not the
  headline. Pages/Ray lead with SOLAR + STORAGE. Mini-split appears only as "what's included" detail.

## Ray (chatbot) — STAY IN THE SOLAR LANE
- Ray is a **residential solar + battery storage** concierge. He talks solar, storage, self-consumption,

- Ray must NOT volunteer heat-pump / mini-split / HVAC as a topic or position VoltSol as an HVAC installer.

## Grid / export visual + copy — LEGALLY DEFENSIBLE (protect against a litigious utility)
RULES:
- Do NOT name any utility (no "PG&E", "SMUD", "SCE", "SDG&E") in the grid-asymmetry argument copy or the
  visual. (Named utility RATE context elsewhere in factual market data is fine, but the "they're screwing
  you" argument must not name a company.)
- Do NOT assert a universal "$0.30 → $0.08" figure as if true for everyone. It is not.
- DO make the TRUE, defensible structural point: under California's current net-billing rules (NEM 3.0),
  **the credit a homeowner receives for solar energy they export is typically only a small fraction of the
  retail price they pay to buy electricity back.** This asymmetry is public CPUC policy fact.
- Hedge all quantities: "often," "many homeowners," "can be," "a fraction of," "as little as." Tie claims
  to the regulatory regime, not a company's choice.
- Allowed framing: "The rules changed. Exporting your solar to the grid now pays a fraction of what you pay
  to buy power back. So the smart move is simple: keep the power you make." No villain named; the POLICY is
  the subject.
- Visual concept: MAKE IT → STORE IT → USE IT (keep ~100% of value) vs. SELL IT CHEAP → BUY IT BACK
  EXPENSIVE (export credit ≪ retail). No utility logos/names, no hard universal cents number.
